What is a HEIC file?

HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is the default image capture format on iPhones running iOS 11 and later. While HEIC is excellent for saving storage space, it is notoriously difficult to open on Windows PCs, Android devices, or web browsers. Converting your photos to JPG makes them universally accessible.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does HEIC conversion work locally?
QuickKit dynamically loads a secure, client-side decoder library (`heic2any.js`) inside your browser to parse the HEIC metadata and extract image arrays. This guarantees that your private camera photos are never sent over the internet or uploaded to a cloud.
Will I lose my metadata or EXIF data?
Converting HEIC to JPG via standard canvas rendering strips embedded EXIF headers (like location coordinates or camera models) to keep files light and secure.
